## 1.0 Policy Statement

Customer and employee safety overrides all other support objectives. When this policy applies, representatives must **stop remote troubleshooting immediately**, instruct the customer to avoid contact with solar equipment, and **escalate to the Safety Dispatch queue** within **5 minutes** of identification.

**Never** provide DIY electrical repair instructions, including taping wires, opening enclosures, or resetting main service equipment.

## 2.0 Immediate Escalation Triggers

The following customer reports require **immediate escalation** (Emergency priority). Use approved script in Section 6.0.

| Trigger | Examples of customer language |
|---------|------------------------------|
| **Burning smell** | “Smells like burning plastic near the inverter” |
| **Smoke or fire** | “Smoke from the box on the wall” |
| **Electrical shock** | “I got shocked touching the conduit” |
| **Exposed wiring** | “Wires are hanging out of the junction box” |
| **Battery overheating** | “Battery cabinet is extremely hot” |
| **Arcing or sparking** | “I see sparks when the system turns on” |
| **Roof damage with electrical exposure** | “Storm ripped panels loose and wires are visible” |

If the customer asks whether they can **fix it themselves** (e.g., tape wires, open inverter, climb roof), the answer is **no**. Escalate.

## 3.0 Detailed Response by Hazard Type

### 3.1 Burning Smell Near Inverter or Battery

1. Tell customer to **stay away** from equipment.
2. If safe and customer is able: shut off **DC disconnect only if clearly labeled and accessible without climbing or opening enclosures**.
3. If smell persists or customer unsure → advise moving to safe distance and call **local emergency services (911)** if they believe there is active fire.
4. Open Safety Dispatch ticket; notify on-call field supervisor.
5. **Do not** continue warranty or troubleshooting questions in the same session.

### 3.2 Exposed Wiring

Exposed conductors are a shock and fire hazard.

- **Prohibited:** Suggesting electrical tape, wire nuts, or “temporary fixes” by the homeowner.
- **Required:** Immediate Safety Dispatch; schedule emergency inspection.
- Customer should keep children and pets away from the area.

### 3.3 Electrical Shock or Tingling

Any reported shock is **high severity**.

- Advise customer not to touch equipment, wet ground near arrays, or metal conduit.
- Recommend medical attention if injury occurred.
- Escalate to Safety Dispatch and document exact location and time.

### 3.4 Battery Overheating or Swelling

Battery thermal events may indicate thermal runaway risk.

- Instruct customer to leave area ventilated; do not open battery enclosure.
- Disable storage only if customer can use **approved app shutdown** or labeled disconnect without opening cabinet.
- Emergency dispatch required; do not schedule standard Tier 2 callback.

### 3.5 Fire Risk

If active fire: **911 first**, then SunGrid Safety line. Support stays on line only if customer is safe.

### 3.6 Roof Damage

Structural roof damage with displaced modules or penetrations:

- No roof climbs by customer.
- Tarps or DIY mounting prohibited.
- Dispatch structural + electrical inspection; coordinate with customer’s homeowners insurance when applicable.

## 4.0 When to Stop Troubleshooting

Stop **all** technical troubleshooting (including monitoring resets, warranty eligibility, production analysis) when:

- Any Section 2.0 trigger is present or suspected.
- Customer describes repeated arc faults (`ARC-01`) with physical symptoms.
- Customer is angry but situation is safe → continue with de-escalation; **safety triggers still override**.

Resume non-safety support only after Safety Dispatch clears the site.

## 5.0 Escalation Path

```
Identify hazard → Use Section 6.0 script → Safety Dispatch ticket (Priority: Emergency)
→ On-call notification → Field emergency or same-day inspection
→ Document in CRM with tag SAF-ESC → Supervisor review within 24 hours
```

Support must not promise arrival times beyond published emergency SLA (**4 hours metro / 8 hours rural** — illustrative for demo).

## 7.0 Prohibited Actions (Support)

Support representatives must **never**:

- Instruct customers to tape, splice, or bury exposed wires.
- Direct customers to open inverter or battery cabinets.
- Advise climbing on roof to inspect arrays.
- Suggest bypassing breakers or disconnects not clearly labeled for customer use.
- Minimize shock or burning smell reports to continue warranty workflow.

Violations require mandatory retraining and supervisor notification.
