## 1.0 Purpose and Scope

This policy defines warranty coverage, exclusions, claim requirements, and escalation criteria for residential solar installations sold and serviced by SunGrid Solutions. Support representatives must not guarantee coverage beyond what is written here. When in doubt, route to Warranty Review per Section 6.3.

## 2.0 Covered Equipment

### 2.1 Panel Performance Warranty

SunGrid-branded and SunGrid-approved modules carry a **25-year linear performance warranty**. At Year 1, modules must produce at least **98%** of rated nameplate capacity under Standard Test Conditions (STC). Annual degradation must not exceed **0.5% per year** thereafter.

**Example:** A 10 kW system rated at 10,000 W STC should not fall below 9,800 W equivalent output in Year 1 under validated test conditions.

### 2.2 Inverter Warranty

String inverters: **12 years** parts and labor when installed by a SunGrid Authorized Installer. Microinverters: **25 years** limited warranty per manufacturer schedule on file in the customer account.

Inverter warranty covers manufacturing defects and failure under normal operating conditions. It does **not** cover damage from improper homeowner resets, unapproved modifications, or storm surge without documented lightning protection.

### 2.3 Battery Storage Warranty

SunGrid Home Battery units: **10 years** or 4,000 cycles (whichever comes first), minimum **70%** retained capacity at end of warranty period when operated within published temperature and depth-of-discharge limits.

## 3.0 Performance Degradation and Production Shortfalls

### 3.1 Normal vs. Abnormal Production Loss

Seasonal variation, shading changes (new trees, structures), soiling, and weather are **not** warranty defects. Support should complete the **Low Energy Output Checklist** in the Installation Troubleshooting Guide (Section 2.1) before opening a warranty claim.

### 3.2 Performance Degradation Thresholds

A production shortfall may qualify for warranty review when **all** of the following are true:

1. Monitoring data shows sustained underproduction for **60+ consecutive days** (not a single cloudy week).
2. Output is **more than 20% below** SunGrid’s expected production model for that site after weather normalization.
3. Troubleshooting per SG-TSG-2024-01 Sections 2.1–2.4 has been completed and documented.
4. No exclusion in Section 4.0 applies.

A customer reporting **35% below expected production** after troubleshooting and sustained underperformance **may** qualify for warranty review. Do **not** promise approval; open a review ticket.

### 3.3 Monitoring Data Requirements

Valid claims require at least **90 days** of monitoring history unless the system is newly commissioned (minimum 30 days post-PTO). Gaps in monitoring must be explained (e.g., communication outage vs. inverter offline).

## 4.0 Warranty Exclusions

Coverage does **not** apply to:

- Damage from fire, flood, earthquake, or other force majeure (unless separate insurance applies).
- Work performed by non-authorized technicians or DIY modifications.
- Pest damage, vandalism, or intentional misuse.
- Failure to maintain accessible roof pathways or vegetation clearance where required by install contract.
- Expected wear on consumables (e.g., Wi-Fi adapters not supplied by SunGrid).
- Production loss caused solely by shading or utility curtailment programs.

If a customer mentions **burning smell, exposed wiring, or shock**, stop warranty discussion and follow **Safety Escalation Policy (SG-SAF-2024-01)** immediately.

## 5.0 Required Claim Documentation

Before submitting to Warranty Review, collect:

| Item | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Customer account ID | 10-digit SunGrid account number |
| Installation date / PTO date | From CRM or contract record |
| Equipment serial numbers | Panels, inverter(s), battery if applicable |
| Monitoring export | 90-day production report (PDF or CSV) |
| Troubleshooting log | Completed checklist from SG-TSG-2024-01 |
| Site photos | Array, inverter label, monitoring screen (if requested) |
| Storm date (if relevant) | For weather-related production disputes |

Support may pre-fill forms; customers should not send equipment serial numbers over unsecured channels without verification per SOP Section 5.2.

## 6.0 Escalation to Warranty Review

### 6.1 When Support May Answer Directly

- General warranty term questions (length, what equipment is covered).
- Status of an **existing** claim ticket.
- Explanation of monitoring vs. warranty (Section 3.3).

### 6.2 When Support Must Open a Warranty Review Ticket

- Sustained underproduction meeting Section 3.2 thresholds.
- Repeated inverter failure after authorized service.
- Battery capacity below 70% within warranty period (with BMS logs).
- Customer disputes exclusion under Section 4.0.

### 6.3 Warranty Review SLA

Warranty Review responds within **5 business days** with accept, deny, or request for additional information. Support must not quote repair timelines until Review confirms.

## 7.0 Communication Standards

Use approved language:

- **Do say:** “Based on our policy, your situation may qualify for warranty review once we complete troubleshooting and gather monitoring data.”
- **Do not say:** “Your panels are definitely covered,” or “We will replace your entire system.”

Refer financing or payment disputes to **Financing FAQ (SG-FIN-2024-01)** — warranty does not govern loan or lease terms.
